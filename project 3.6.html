6. CONCLUSION

Designing Eatzy helped me understand that great UI/UX design goes beyond choosing nice colors or attractive layouts. The real goal of this project was to create a simple, focused, and easy way for people to order food without distractions.

I intentionally designed the app to deliver food only, removing unnecessary categories like groceries or pharmacy items. This allowed me to focus on clarity, speed, and ease of use.

Every design decision — from the navigation structure to the customization flow — was made to reduce confusion and improve the user experience.

The project took me several weeks to complete, from research and user understanding to wireframing and high-fidelity design. During this process, I learned that UI/UX design requires patience, problem-solving, and attention to detail. It is not just about how things look, but about how they work and how users feel when interacting with them.

This project strengthened my understanding that good design is about making digital products more user-friendly, efficient, and purposeful. It reinforced my passion for creating solutions that genuinely improve everyday experiences.
